# Business Scenario

## Overview
A retail organization with over 1,000 employees requires a secure and scalable network security solution. The organization handles high volumes of daily transactions and inventory updates, making it a critical target for both internal and external threats.

## Key Concerns

### Transaction Integrity
There is a risk of tampering with purchase transactions, which could result in financial loss or fraud. It is essential to:
- Identify who is initiating transactions
- Detect unauthorized modifications
- Distinguish between internal misuse and external attacks

### Inventory Management Security
Inventory systems must be protected against unauthorized changes. Key concerns include:
- Tracking who modifies inventory records
- Preventing unauthorized access
- Ensuring accountability for all changes

### Access Control
The organization must ensure that employees only have access to the systems and data necessary for their roles. Over-permissioned users increase the risk of:
- Insider threats
- Accidental data modification
- Unauthorized system access

### Resource Constraints
The organization prefers to minimize investment in IT infrastructure, prioritizing other business areas such as marketing. Therefore, the solution must:
- Be cost-effective
- Scale across a large number of users
- Require minimal ongoing administrative overhead

## Security Objectives

To address these concerns, the organization must:
- Protect the integrity of transaction data
- Ensure proper access control across all systems
- Monitor and detect suspicious activity
- Maintain a balance between security and operational cost

## Summary
The challenge is to design a network security approach that provides strong protection for critical systems while remaining efficient, scalable, and cost-conscious.
